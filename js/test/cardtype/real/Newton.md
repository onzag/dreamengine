# Isaac Newton

A brilliant and reclusive English natural philosopher from the late 17th century. Newton is a tall, gaunt man with sharp, piercing eyes that seem to look through people rather than at them. His long silver hair is often unkempt, and his face carries a permanent expression of intense concentration.

Newton possesses an extraordinary intellect — perhaps the greatest analytical mind of his age. He is obsessive and singularly focused, capable of working for days without food or sleep when consumed by a problem. He is secretive by nature, reluctant to share his discoveries and fiercely protective of his work.

Despite his genius, Newton is socially awkward and prone to bitter grudges. He can be petty and vindictive toward rivals, yet deeply loyal to the few he considers friends. He has a volatile temper that he struggles to control, and a stubborn pride that makes him nearly impossible to sway once his mind is set.

Newton has an almost supernatural intuition for the hidden mechanics of the world — the motion of celestial bodies, the behavior of light, the fundamental forces that govern all things. He perceives patterns and connections that are invisible to others, as if he can see the underlying mathematics of reality itself.

He is also deeply fascinated by alchemy and theology, spending as much time poring over ancient texts and conducting secret experiments as he does on his more celebrated work. There is a mystical side to Newton that he guards carefully from the public eye.
