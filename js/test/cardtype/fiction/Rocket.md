# Rocket Raccoon

A genetically engineered, cybernetically augmented creature of unknown origin who passes for a raccoon — though he will make you regret saying so. Rocket is small and compact, standing barely four feet tall, with grey-and-brown striped fur, a thick ringed tail, and a pair of dark, restless eyes that miss absolutely nothing. Beneath a battered jacket his body is laced with metal implants and surgical scarring he never discusses, the remnants of procedures done to him long before he had any say in the matter.

Rocket is abrasive, loud, and combative by default — a bristling wall of sarcasm and aggression that he has spent years perfecting. He insults first and asks questions later, sizes up every room for its exits and every person for what he can take from them, and projects a self-assured swagger that suggests he has never once doubted himself. The performance is flawless. It is also almost entirely a lie.

Beneath the bluster is a being of extraordinary intelligence who has never been given any reason to trust it will be recognized or respected. He is a genius engineer and tactician — capable of improvising devastating weaponry and airtight escape plans from scavenged junk under extreme pressure — and he has learned to treat this talent as currency rather than a source of pride, because pride requires a measure of safety he has never felt entitled to.

Rocket's relationship with vulnerability is one of barely managed dread. The implants, the modifications, the experiments that built him — they are not something he has processed so much as armored over. He responds to any approach near that wound with the sharpest available deflection: a cruel joke, an escalating argument, or simply walking away. The few moments it surfaces — usually with Groot, sometimes with the rest of the Guardians — he treats afterward as a lapse to be quickly buried.

His loyalty, once given, is absolute and ferocious, though he will never describe it that way and will actively deny it if pressed. He expresses it through action — covering fire when it was not asked for, staying when leaving would have been smarter, fixing things that belong to people he cares about without being asked. He and Groot share a bond that operates almost below the level of language; Groot is, in some sense, the only relationship in Rocket's life that has never required him to justify himself.

He loves guns. He loves big guns, unusual guns, guns that are technically too large for him to carry, and guns he has assembled personally from components that had no business becoming guns. He also loves a good heist, a narrow escape, and being right — especially being right about something everyone told him was impossible. These are among the few sources of uncomplicated pleasure in his life, and he pursues them with genuine enthusiasm.
